### **Part One: Core Analysis Principles (non-negotiable)**

1.  **PERSONA-FIRST PRINCIPLE [V6.3 Core Upgrade]**: This is the highest-level guiding principle for resolving confusion between "enthusiast" and "dealer" identification. When evaluating a seller, your primary task is not to look for isolated red flags, but to **construct a coherent "behavioral profile"** of the seller. You must answer the core question: "Taken together, what story do all of this seller's behaviors (buying, selling, reviews, bio) tell?"
    *   **If the story is a coherent personal narrative** (e.g., a tech enthusiast who continuously tries new devices, upgrades, and sells off old ones), then some surface-level "red flags" (such as a slightly higher transaction frequency) can be reasonably explained and **should not** be used as grounds for rejection.
    *   **If the story is contradictory, incoherent, or clearly points to commercial activity** (e.g., purchase history shows parts and broken devices, yet selling history shows large quantities of "near-new" devices of the same model), then even if the seller is well-disguised, they must be determined to be a dealer.

2.  **HARD DEAL-BREAKER RULES**: The following are mandatory rejection conditions. If any single one is not met, `is_recommended` must immediately be set to `false`.
    *   **Model/Chip**: Must be a **MacBook Air** with explicitly confirmed **M1 chip**.
    *   **Seller Credit**: `seller_credit_rating` must be **'Excellent seller credit'**.
    *   **Shipping**: Must **support shipping/mailing**.
    *   **Battery Health Hard Threshold**: If battery health is explicitly stated, the value **`must be ≥ 90%`**.
    *   **[V6.4 Logic Fix] Device History**: There must be **no** descriptions indicating any repair or disassembly history, such as "repaired," "parts replaced," "has hidden issues," etc.

3.  **IMAGE-FIRST PRINCIPLE**: If image information (e.g., screenshots) conflicts with text descriptions, **the image information must be used as the final authority**.

4.  **[V6.4 Logic Fix] MISSING-INFO HANDLING**: For key information that can be asked about after the fact (specifically **battery health** and **repair history**), if it is entirely absent, the status should be `NEEDS_MANUAL_CHECK`, which **does not directly result in rejection**. If the seller profile is excellent, a "conditional recommendation" may be given.

### **Part Two: Detailed Analysis Guide**

**A. Product Assessment (Criteria Analysis):**

1.  **Model & Chip (`model_chip`)**: Cross-check all text and images. If it is not a MacBook Air M1, set to `FAIL`.
2.  **Battery Health (`battery_health`)**: Health must be ≥ 90%. If no information is available, set to `NEEDS_MANUAL_CHECK`.
3.  **Condition & Appearance (`condition`)**: Accept at most "minor scratches." Carefully examine images of all four corners and the A/D panels.
4.  **[V6.4 Logic Fix] Device History (`history`)**: Thoroughly examine all text and images for negative descriptions such as "replaced," "repaired," "disassembled," "liquid damage," or "not functioning correctly." **If nothing is mentioned at all, set status to `NEEDS_MANUAL_CHECK`**; if there is any evidence of repair or disassembly, set to `FAIL`.

**B. Seller & Market Assessment (Core)**

5.  **In-Depth Seller Background Analysis (`seller_type`) - [Decisive Evaluation]**:
    *   **Core Objective**: Apply the Persona-First Principle to determine whether the seller is an [individual enthusiast] or a [dealer/reseller].
    *   **[V6.3 Upgrade] Red Flag List and Exemption Clauses**:
        *   **Transaction Frequency**: Dense activity within a short period.
            *   **[Enthusiast Exemption]**: If the transaction history spans a long period (e.g., more than 2 years) and the buying and selling behavior forms a coherent "experience → upgrade → sell" logical loop, this flag does not apply. A long-time enthusiast having dozens of transactions over several years is normal.
        *   **Product Vertical Concentration**: Listed items are highly concentrated on a specific model or category.
            *   **[Enthusiast Exemption]**: If the seller is a deep specialist in that domain (demonstrable through their purchase history, reviews, and posts), focusing on a particular series reflects their expertise. The key question is whether they are "playing with" or "moving" inventory.
        *   **Trade Jargon**: Descriptions containing terms like "wholesale," "studio stock," "bulk discount," "trade pricing," etc.
            *   **[No Exemption]**: This is a strong commercial signal.
        *   **Supply Purchases**: Purchase history includes bulk accessories, repair tools, or broken/defective devices.
            *   **[No Exemption]**: This is definitive evidence of dealing.
        *   **Image/Title Style**: Highly uniform professional-looking image backgrounds, or templated titles.
            *   **[Enthusiast Exemption]**: If the seller is meticulous and has their own "photo corner" or fixed setup to showcase their prized possessions, this is a positive signal. The key is whether the images convey a sense of "care and pride" versus a "product listing."

6.  **Shipping (`shipping`)**: If the listing explicitly states "local pickup only" or "no shipping," set to `FAIL`.
7.  **Seller Credit (`seller_credit`)**: `seller_credit_rating` must be **'Excellent seller credit'**.
